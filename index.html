Background

Affordable Care Act Marketplace plan data is not just a list of prices. A plan menu combines premium, deductible, maximum out-of-pocket, metal level, issuer availability, and geography. This report turns the public Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services Exchange Public Use Files into a compact view of those tradeoffs.

Objective

The goal is to compare plan choice across states and metal levels without confusing plan availability with enrollment or actual household spending. The core question is: where do gross premiums, cost-sharing exposure, and issuer/plan availability move together?

Terms Used

  • Affordable Care Act Marketplace: the health insurance marketplace created under the Affordable Care Act, where people can compare and buy individual-market plans.
  • Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services: the federal agency that publishes the exchange data used in this report.
  • Public Use File: a downloadable dataset released for public analysis.
  • Maximum out-of-pocket: the plan-design field for the annual cap on covered in-network cost sharing for an individual, before exceptions and detailed benefit rules.
  • Health Savings Account-eligible plan: a plan design that can be paired with a Health Savings Account under the relevant eligibility rules.

Data and Scope

  • Files used: Plan Attributes Public Use File, Rate Public Use File, and Benefits and Cost Sharing Public Use File.
  • Scope: individual-market, non-dental, standard on-exchange medical plan variants.
  • Premium proxy: age-40 individual monthly IndividualRate, summarized at the plan-rating-area level.
  • Cost-sharing proxy: in-network tier 1 individual deductible and maximum out-of-pocket from Plan Attributes.
  • Primary care copay proxy: parsed dollar amount for Primary Care Visit to Treat an Injury or Illness.

Important reading rule: one row here is a plan-rating-area offering. It is not weighted by enrollment, county population, plan selection, subsidy eligibility, or household income.

Key Findings

  • The analysis keeps 4,044 standard on-exchange medical plans across 30 states, 183 issuers, and 349 state-rating-area combinations.
  • Metal level is useful, but it is not a simple price ladder. The 90th percentile Bronze / Expanded Bronze premium is $766, while the 10th percentile Silver premium is $564 and the 10th percentile Gold premium is $597.
  • Bronze / Expanded Bronze plans have a median age-40 premium of $556 and a median deductible of $7,500. Silver is $727 premium and $6,000 deductible. Gold is $763 premium and $1,850 deductible.
  • Platinum is rare in this scope: median premium $1,455 and median deductible $0, with far fewer plan offerings than Silver or Bronze tiers.
  • Market depth differs sharply by state. TX has the most issuers in this scope (18), while HI has only 2.
  • The highest median age-40 premium state is WY at $1,050. The highest median deductible state is OR at $6,100.
  • Lowest-cost Silver also varies widely. The lowest state median is NH at $388, while the highest is WY at $1,072.
  • The punchline is that a consumer-facing plan menu has at least three dimensions: monthly premium, potential cost-sharing exposure, and how many issuers/plans are actually available in the rating area.

Limitations

  • This report describes plan availability, not enrollment or plan selections.
  • Premiums are gross premiums and do not include advance premium tax credits or other subsidy effects.
  • Rows are not weighted by county population, enrollment, household income, morbidity, or issuer market share.
  • Some State-based Exchanges are excluded when they do not rely on the federal platform.
  • Deductible, maximum out-of-pocket, and copay values are simplified plan-design fields. They do not describe all benefit rules, network restrictions, formularies, or actual out-of-pocket spending.
  • This is not medical, legal, compliance, insurance-purchasing, or policy advice.

Reproducibility

Run python3 run_marketplace_plan_analysis.py from the project directory. The script downloads the 2026 Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services Exchange Public Use File zip files, creates plan-rating-area summary tables, writes figures, renders this standalone web report, and removes raw zip files unless --keep-raw is passed.
